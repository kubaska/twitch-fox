Twitch Fox, version 4.4.1 (fork)

Welcome to Twitch Fox!

You can browse Twitch by clicking the tabs on the left, and search Twitch for content relating to the current tab by using the (currently not visible) search bar above. Want to quickly view and receive notifications for followed content? Click 'Log In' in the top right corner to connect with Twitch! And don't forget to check out the settings by clicking the gear icon in the bottom left corner, where you can disable tooltips and much more.

Whether you have used Twitch Fox in the past or have downloaded it for the first time, I hope you enjoy my add-on!

About Twitch Fox

Hey! You are using forked version of Twitch Fox. I used this addon for well over 2 years now and loved it however it lacked feature that i really wanted: ability to follow channels locally while also being logged into Twitch. So after inspecting the codebase few times in 2020 I decided to finally make it happen.

This fork is far from perfect and extension needs a full proper rewrite, preferably on some reactive framework. That being said I decided against it, because Twitch Kraken API is already deprecated and their new Helix API would make it much, much more difficult to port all the awesome features this extension has.

What is Twitch Fox? This is a Firefox add-on I originally made in April 2015 under the name 'Twitch Alarm,' and it has grown from a simple followed stream notifier to a full-fledged Twitch browser. I've worked on it in small and dedicated spurts when I had free time throughout the years, and I've learned a lot from developing it.

Like Twitch Fox? Please leave a review on the add-on page! If you really really like it, please consider donating. Twitch Fox is the product of many hours of free time, and any contribution is greatly appreciated! Have a problem or suggestion? Leave an issue on the GitHub page or contact me directly, and I will try to get around to fixing it. Thank you for using my add-on!
